Carrots are root vegetables that are typically orange, though they can also be purple, red, yellow, or white. They are crunchy and slightly sweet, making them popular both raw and cooked. Carrots are rich in beta-carotene and vitamin A.

Broccoli is a green vegetable that belongs to the cabbage family. It has a tree-like structure with a thick stalk and flowering head. Broccoli is packed with vitamins C and K, and is often steamed, roasted, or eaten raw in salads.

Spinach is a leafy green vegetable that is incredibly nutritious. It can be eaten raw in salads or cooked in various dishes. Spinach is rich in iron, calcium, and vitamins A and C. It has a mild, slightly earthy flavor.

Bell peppers come in various colors including green, red, yellow, and orange. Red bell peppers are the sweetest and most mature. They are crunchy and can be eaten raw, roasted, or cooked in stir-fries and other dishes.

Cucumbers are long, green vegetables with a mild, refreshing taste. They have a high water content and are often eaten raw in salads or as a healthy snack. Cucumbers are low in calories and provide hydration.
