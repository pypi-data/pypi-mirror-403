# Tone & Style Guidelines

## Tone Selection Guide

### Formal Tone
**Use when:**
- Communicating with executives or senior stakeholders
- Legal or compliance matters
- Formal complaints or escalations
- Official announcements

**Characteristics:**
- Professional, respectful language
- Complete sentences, proper grammar
- No contractions
- Formal greetings and closings

**Example:** "Dear [Name], We acknowledge receipt of your correspondence dated [date] regarding [matter]. We are currently reviewing this matter and will provide a comprehensive response within [timeframe]."

### Professional Tone
**Use when:**
- Business-to-business communications
- Customer service interactions
- Standard inquiries and responses
- Most common business scenarios

**Characteristics:**
- Clear, direct language
- Friendly but professional
- May use contractions sparingly
- Balanced formality

**Example:** "Hello [Name], Thank you for contacting us about [topic]. We're happy to help and have the following information to share..."

### Casual Tone
**Use when:**
- Internal team communications
- Established relationships
- Informal inquiries
- Non-critical matters

**Characteristics:**
- Conversational language
- Contractions acceptable
- Friendly, approachable
- Less formal structure

**Example:** "Hi [Name], Thanks for reaching out! Here's what I can tell you about [topic]..."

### Friendly Tone
**Use when:**
- Customer appreciation messages
- Positive interactions
- Relationship building
- Thank you messages

**Characteristics:**
- Warm, personable language
- Enthusiastic but professional
- Shows genuine interest
- Positive framing

**Example:** "Hi [Name], I hope you're doing well! I'm excited to share that [positive news]..."

## Language Guidelines

### Do's
- Use active voice when possible
- Be specific and concrete
- Show empathy and understanding
- Use positive framing
- Include clear calls to action

### Don'ts
- Avoid jargon unless necessary
- Don't make promises you can't keep
- Avoid defensive language
- Don't use all caps (except for emphasis)
- Avoid overly technical language without explanation

## Urgency Indicators

### High Urgency
- "We're prioritizing this matter"
- "We'll address this immediately"
- "This is being escalated to our priority queue"

### Medium Urgency
- "We'll get back to you within [timeframe]"
- "We're working on this and will update you soon"
- "This is in progress"

### Low Urgency
- "We'll review this and respond"
- "Thank you for your patience"
- "We appreciate you bringing this to our attention"
